## Opening Ceremony — HG01 Birley, Centenary Building — 09:00

- Welcome everyone to the City St George's Clinical AI Hackathon.
- This week, over 100 participants have come together to tackle a real problem facing the NHS.
- The challenge: cancer patients are discussed in weekly Multidisciplinary Team meetings — MDTs. The outcomes of those meetings are recorded in Word documents, but extracting that data for research or audit is laborious and largely manual. The goal this week has been to build a tool that extracts structured patient data from those MDT documents automatically, reducing the burden on clinicians and unlocking data for research.
- The problem was brought to us by Dr Anita Wale, Consultant Oncologist at St George's Hospital, and the synthetic dataset was curated by Ellie Hickey — thank you both.
- Thank you to Dr Alex Nicholls from the Ministry of Defence for his guidance on technical and regulatory considerations, and to Hitesh Patel from the NHS for bridging the clinical and technical domains throughout this project.
- I would like to introduce our judging panel: Dr Anita Wale, NHS; Dr Alex Nicholls, Ministry of Defence; and Hitesh Patel, NHS. They will be evaluating your presentations today.
- Teams have been working across the week — through workshops on AI Agents, LLM APIs, and documentation — and some of you have been here since last night. That dedication is remarkable.
- This morning we will hear presentations from each team, followed by judging and awards this afternoon.
- Good luck to all participants — now let's see what you've built.
- Teams, please now make your way to your allocated presentation room — details are in the README on the GitHub repository.
